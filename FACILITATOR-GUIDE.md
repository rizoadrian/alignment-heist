## Run-of-Show Script

### Opening (2 min)
> "Welcome. For the next 30 minutes we're going to do something useful with our 24 goals — map them to the four Technology strategic initiatives. This tells us how our work connects up the chain and where we might be over- or under-invested.
>
> Here's how it works: I draw a card — a goal — it appears on your screen and the projector. You have 30 seconds to vote silently: pick a primary initiative, and optionally a secondary. No discussion yet. Then we see the results and I decide if we debate or just move on.
>
> Your code names are on your screens. You're operatives. I'm The Fixer. Let's go."

---

### Per-Card Flow

**Each round takes 20–90 seconds depending on how much debate you allow.**

1. **Draw the card** — click ◆ Draw Card on your facilitator screen
2. **Read it aloud** if the projector text is small
3. **"30 seconds — vote now"** — operatives select primary + optional secondary and lock in
4. **Watch the vote table** on your facilitator screen as votes come in
5. **Call it:** quick commit or open debate (see pacing guide below)
6. **Select primary (+ optional secondary)** on your facilitator screen, click **◆ Commit & Advance**
7. The goal appears on the projected board — brief acknowledgment, move to next card

---

### Pacing Guide — When to Quick Commit vs. Open Debate

**Use ⚡ Quick Commit (and say "any objections? no? next") when:**
- 7+ of 9 votes land on the same initiative
- The goal is operationally obvious (e.g., a data engineering or tooling goal)
- The room is tracking and energy is high — keep momentum

**Open brief debate (60–90 sec max) when:**
- Votes are split 4–5 or more evenly across two initiatives
- A Wild Card is drawn — these are designed for longer discussion
- Someone raises a hand or looks visibly puzzled
- The goal touches governance, strategy, or cross-team ownership

**Debate script:**
> "I'm seeing a split — mostly [III] with some [II]. [Name], you voted [II] — what are you seeing that others might not?"
> *(After 60 seconds)* "Good. I'm going to call this [III] primary, [II] secondary. Committed."

**If the room is going long:**
> "I'm going to make a call and we can revisit in the notes. [Initiative] — committed. Moving on."

---

### Wild Cards

Three wild cards are shuffled into the deck at roughly the 1/3, 2/3, and near-end marks. They look different on screen (striped gold header). When one appears:

**Wild Card A — "The 90-Day Sprint"**
> "Pause. This one's different. Of everything we've mapped so far, if we could only protect one goal in the first 90 days — what is it? Call it out."
> *(Take 2-3 answers, then)* "Let's vote it against the initiatives."

**Wild Card B — "The Blind Spot"**
> "Look at the board. One of those four initiatives might be underrepresented. Is that because our goals don't touch it — or because we're not being honest about what we actually do?"
> *(Open 90-second discussion, then vote the wild card to an initiative as a placeholder.)*

**Wild Card C — "The Risk Check"**
> "Last strategic question: which goal on that board is most at risk of not happening this year? And if we're honest — does it still deserve where we put it?"
> *(Open 2-minute discussion. Capture key comments in your notes field.)*

## Troubleshooting

**Someone's link doesn't work / wrong screen:**
- Make sure they're using the exact URL from the table above — no extra spaces
- Have them try in Chrome or Safari if another browser is acting up
- In a pinch, they can pair with a neighbor and vote verbally through you

**App shows an error or goes blank:**
- Hard refresh: ⌘+Shift+R (Mac) or Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows)
- If the facilitator screen breaks, reload your tab — Firebase will restore state instantly

**Votes aren't showing up:**
- Check WiFi — all devices need internet connectivity
- Ask affected operatives to reload their tab

**Running long:**
- Switch entirely to Quick Commit for remaining cards — just announce "I'm calling this, we can revisit"
- Skip the third Wild Card if it hasn't appeared yet (you can always discuss it verbally)
- Target: 24 goal cards + 3 wild cards at ~45 sec average = ~25 minutes of card time

**Someone voted wrong / wants to change:**
- Votes are locked once submitted — tell them their rationale can go in the notes
- You as facilitator always make the canonical call anyway

**Session needs to be reset (for a dry run or if something goes wrong):**
- Click ↺ Reset on your facilitator screen — clears everything and starts fresh
- Re-initialize and draw the first card to restart
